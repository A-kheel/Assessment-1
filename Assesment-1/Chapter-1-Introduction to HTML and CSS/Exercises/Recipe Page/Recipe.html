Bulalo Recipe

Bulalo is a traditional Filipino beef soup made by simmering beef shank and bone marrow for several hours until the meat becomes tender and the broth develops a rich, savory flavor. This dish is especially popular in the cooler regions of the Philippines, such as Tagaytay and Batangas, where it is commonly served hot and enjoyed with rice and dipping sauces like fish sauce with calamansi and chili. Its comforting and hearty nature makes it a favorite for family gatherings and rainy days.

Ingredients

  • 2 beef marrow bones
  • 1 pound beef shank
  • 1 onion(quartered)
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 1 teaspoon of black peppercorns
  • 2 tablespoons of patis
  • salt
  • 2 corn cobs
  • 1 chayote(peeled and cubed)
  • 3 baby bok choy

Instructions:

  1. Boil a large pot of water. Add the marrow bones and beef shank and return to a boil. Continue boiling until you don't see any more red blood coming from the meat or bones (about 10 minutes), then remove the meat and bones with tongs and scrub under cold water to remove any scum. Dump the water in the pot out and rinse the pot. This process rids the meat of excess blood and will ensure your soup is nice and clear.

  2. Return the cleaned meat and bones to the pot then add the onion, garlic, peppercorns and patis. Cover with water then bring it to a rolling boil and skim off any scum that accumulates.

  3. Reduce the heat to medium low. If you are using a pressure cooker, afix the lid and let it cook for 1 Â½ hours. If you're not using a pressure cooker, simmer until the meat on the shank is fork tender (4-5 hours). Skim off any excessive fat from the top but do not remove it all (remember, fat=flavour). Transfer the meat and bones to a bowl, then strain the stock through a fine mesh sieve, discard the solids then return the meat and bones to the strained stock.

  4. Add the corn and chayote and simmer for another 20 minutes or until the chayote is tender. Salt to taste, then add the bok choy at the last minute. Serve with rice(IMPORTANT).
